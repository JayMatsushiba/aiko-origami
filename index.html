About Me

When I was a young girl in Japan, my grandmother taught me how to make an origami crane. I found it magical that a single piece of paper can be transformed into something beautiful. I left Japan over 20 years ago, since then I have raised three children here in Canada. I made sure to pass down my knowledge in origami to my children as well.

In order to make ends meet, I ran a childcare at home. Through this, I realized that sharing origami is wonderful. I became more interested in doing origami workshops for community centers and libraries.

I became an artist through my grandmother’s inspiration and my passion to share the wonder that a simple piece of paper can become beautiful.
